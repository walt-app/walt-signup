# Walt Design Context

## Users

Privacy-conscious smartphone users across Europe (Nordic focus initially), spanning all ages and demographics. They use tap-to-pay daily and want the same convenience without Google or Apple harvesting their transaction data. They're joining a waitlist now; the app charges a simple 10 EUR/year membership.

## Brand Personality

**Principled, calm, honest.** Walt speaks with quiet confidence. No hype, no marketing theatrics. The brand earns trust through transparency and restraint — saying less, meaning more. The tone is direct and conversational, never corporate or salesy.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Visual tone**: Clean and minimal, inspired by Linear's precision and clarity. Generous whitespace, tight typography, intentional use of color.
- **Color philosophy**: Color should be used sparingly and with purpose — emphasis and CTAs, not decoration. The palette should feel calm and grounded, not loud or attention-grabbing.
- **Typography**: Geist font family. Tight letter-spacing for headings. Large, confident headings. Body text is readable and comfortable.
- **Theme**: Light mode only.
- **Anti-references**: No generic SaaS aesthetics (gradient blobs, stock photos, cookie-cutter layouts). No corporate/enterprise feel (blue suits, formal tone, excessive formality). No flashy fintech vibes.

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint over decoration** — Every element must earn its place. Prefer removing over adding. White space is a feature, not wasted space.
2. **Clarity over cleverness** — Copy and UI should be immediately understood. No jargon, no ambiguity. If it needs explanation, simplify it.
3. **Trust through transparency** — The design should feel honest and open. No dark patterns, no manipulative urgency. Let the product speak for itself.
4. **Inclusive by default** — WCAG AA compliance as baseline. Support prefers-reduced-motion. Ensure readability across ages and abilities. Semantic HTML throughout.
5. **Substance over style** — Functionality and content come first. Animation and visual flair serve comprehension, never distract from it.
